## 1. Steve Jobs 去世后的内心独白

### 1.1 2011年苹果园区追悼会发言（一手，可信度：高）

**来源**：Fortune / Hacker News转载，2011年10月24日
**URL**：https://fortune.com/2011/10/24/jonathan-ive-on-steve-jobs-and-the-fragility-of-ideas/

【他说的】关于Jobs的即兴构想方式：
> "Steve used to say to me — and he used to say this a lot — 'Hey Jony, here's a dopey idea.'"
> "And sometimes they were. Really dopey. Sometimes they were truly dreadful."
> "But sometimes they took the air from the room and they left us both completely silent."
> "Bold, crazy, magnificent ideas. Or quiet simple ones, which in their subtlety, their detail, they were utterly profound."

【他说的】关于创意的脆弱性（这是他对Jobs最完整的公开评价之一）：
> "You see, I think he better than anyone understood that while ideas ultimately can be so powerful, they begin as fragile, barely formed thoughts, so easily missed, so easily compromised, so easily just squished."

【记者描述的】这段话是Ive在Jobs去世约两周后的追悼会上公开讲述的，是他在公开场合第一次谈论两人的创意合作方式。

### 1.2 WSJ Magazine 悼念文章（一手，可信度：最高）

**来源**：Wall Street Journal Magazine，2021年10月5日（Jobs逝世十周年）
**URL（转述）**：https://9to5mac.com/2021/10/04/jony-ive-pens-wsj-magazine-piece-in-remembrance-of-steve-jobs-i-think-about-steve-every-day/
**注**：本文由Ive本人撰写，非记者采访

【他说的】关于沉默十年：
> "Since giving Steve's eulogy I have not spoken publicly about our friendship, our adventures or our collaboration."
> "But I think about Steve every day."

【他说的】关于日常关系的质地：
> "We had lunch together most days and spent our afternoons in the sanctuary of the design studio. Those were some of the happiest, most creative and joyful times of my life. I loved how he saw the world."
> "The way he thought was profoundly beautiful."

【他说的】关于Jobs的思维方式：
> "He was without doubt the most inquisitive human I have ever met. Steve was preoccupied with the nature and quality of his own thinking. He expected so much of himself and worked hard to think with rare vitality, elegance and discipline."
> "Being curious and exploring tentative ideas were far more important to Steve than being socially acceptable."
> "For Steve, wanting to learn was far more important than wanting to be right."
> "Our curiosity united us."

【他说的】关于自己离开苹果：
> "After nearly 30 years, I left Apple, driven by my curiosity to learn and discover new ways to make a useful contribution."
> "Laurene and I at last are working together. In truth, we have been working together for decades."

### 1.3 BBC Desert Island Discs，2025年2月（二手，可信度：高）

**来源**：BBC Radio 4，主持人 Lauren Laverne，2025年2月播出
**URL（转述）**：https://9to5mac.com/2025/02/24/jony-ive-apple-jobs-bbc/
**Spotify**：https://open.spotify.com/episode/7x65a0stKotgg5YkfOXjjB

**背景**：Desert Island Discs是BBC经典节目，被采访者选8首音乐+1本书+1件奢侈品，在个人叙述框架下谈人生。Ive选曲包括Bananarama & Fun Boy Three的"Really Saying Something"、Simple Minds的"Don't You (Forget About Me)"、德彪西"月光"、U2的"40"。书选了P.G. Wodehouse的Jeeves & Wooster全集，奢侈品是一张床。

【他说的】Jobs临终叮嘱与Ive自嘲式承认：
> Jobs告诉他："I really don't want you to be thinking 'Well, what would Steve do?'"
> Ive的回应："And every time I think, 'I wonder what Steve would do?' I think, 'Ha! I'm doing exactly what you didn't want.'"

【他说的】关于两人之间无声的理解：
> "It was remarkable that, despite the limitations of my ability to communicate, Steve understood what I thought and how I felt."

【他说的】关于iPhone造成的非预期伤害：
> "The nature of innovation is there will be unpredicted consequences, and I celebrate and am encouraged by the very positive contribution, the empowerment, the liberty that is provided to so many people in so many ways."
> "Just because the not-so-positive consequences weren't intended, that doesn't matter relative to how I feel responsible and is a contributor to decisions that I have made since and decisions that I'm making in the future."

【他说的】关于AI带来的新感受（这是少见的他主动表达"兴奋"的场合）：
> "It's the first time in my career that I feel inspired by capability in this way."

【记者描述的】在谈到Jobs时，采访者报道Ive明显表现出情绪。他还表达了对自己也可能被智能手机分散注意力的担忧，称"你需要非常特别的决心和纪律，才不会被吸进去"。

## 2. The New Yorker 深度人物报道（2015）

**来源**：Ian Parker，The New Yorker，2015年2月23日刊，约17,000字
**URL**：https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2015/02/23/shape-things-come
**可信度**：二手（记者转述直接采访），可信度：高

**背景**：Parker获得多次专访权，包括iPhone发布当天、Ive工作室内、新园区建设现场。这是Ive在世时接受的最详细单篇报道。

【他说的】关于他与Jobs第一次见面的非凡感觉：
> "I can't really remember that happening really ever before, meeting somebody when it's just like that"（说完用手指打了个响指）

【他说的】关于Jobs第一次参观设计工作室时的话：
> "Fuck, you've not been very effective, have you?"
（这是Jobs对Ive的半褒半讽——他看出了工作室的价值，但批评Ive没能让公司管理层看到）

【他说的】关于Jobs的专注哲学：
> "Jony, you have to understand there are measures of focus, and one of them is how often you say no."

【他说的】关于失去Jobs之后的表达困境：
> "My intuition's good, but my ability to articulate what I feel was not very good—and remains not very good, frustratingly. And that's what's hard, with Steve not being here now."

【他说的】关于Apple Watch的方形设计决策：
> "When a huge part of the function is lists — a circle doesn't make any sense."

【他说的】关于Watch的整体设计意图：
> "The strangely familiar."（这是他给Watch项目设定的设计目标）

【他说的】关于iPhone 6尺寸的反复权衡：
> "The first one we really felt good about was a 5.7. And then, sleeping on it, and coming back to it, it was just 'Ah, that's way too big.' And then 5.6 still seems too big."

【他说的】关于摄像头凸起（camera bump）：
> "A really very pragmatic optimization."（承认这是功能优先于美观的妥协）

【他说的】关于Walter Isaacson写的Jobs传记：
> "My regard couldn't be any lower."（Parker描述他说这话时有"unusual heat"——罕见的激动语气）

【他说的】关于竞争对手的设计哲学（评论Android可定制策略）：
> "Their value proposition was 'Make it whatever you want.' I believe that's abdicating your responsibility as a designer."

【他说的】评论某款汽车设计：
> "It is baffling, isn't it? It's just nothing, isn't it? It's just insipid."

【记者描述的】Parker观察到，Ive在谈到Jobs时会变得明显沉默。那一年（2015年）被他描述为加入苹果以来"最困难的一年"。Parker还注意到，Ive的办公室里有一张与Steve的合照。

【我推断的】Ive对Isaacson传记的愤怒，不只是因为书里有事实错误，而是因为书把Jobs描述成一个粗暴的人，而Ive认为这伤害了Jobs作为思想家的形象——这与他对"ideas require tenderness"的信念相关。

## 3. Charlie Rose 访谈（2015–2016）

### 3.1 CBS 60 Minutes，2015年12月

**来源**：CBS News/60 Minutes，主持人 Charlie Rose，2015年12月20日播出
**URL（转述）**：https://www.idownloadblog.com/2015/12/20/inside-apple-60-minutes-charlie-rose/

**背景**：Rose获得进入Ive设计工作室的权限（这是极少对外公开的地方）。同期采访还有Tim Cook、Angela Ahrendts等人。

### 3.2 Charlie Rose 独立专访，2016年3月

**来源**：charlierose.com，三段式访谈，2016年3月7–9日播出
**URL**：https://charlierose.com/videos/25315 / https://charlierose.com/videos/24652 / https://charlierose.com/videos/25285
**可信度**：二手（节目转述），来源可靠

【他说的】关于苹果的职责层级：
> "We're very clear about the hierarchies here. Our job isn't to make money for Apple. Our job is to make the best products that we can. We trust if they are good and we trust if we are competent and we do our jobs in trying to describe them and we are competent in making them, they will be attractive and bought."

【他说的】关于产品作为信念的载体：
> "The products are the physical manifestation of a set of believes."

【他说的】关于自我认同（混合身份，不单一）：
> "I think I'm part artist, part designer, part engineer, part builder, and part craftsman. But in all of that, I'm comfortable with being surprised and being wrong and I'm the first person to raise my hand when something I thought would be good was actually appalling."

【他说的】关于创新需要"反理性"：
> "You have to reject reason to innovate."
（这是他少见的强硬表述，通常他更倾向柔和的措辞）

【他说的】关于自我批评的重要性：
> "It's important to be very self-critical."

## 4. The New Yorker TechFest，2017年10月

**来源**：The New Yorker TechFest，主持人 David Remnick，2017年10月6日
**URL（转述）**：https://9to5mac.com/2017/10/06/jony-ive-new-yorker-techfest-live/
**注**：完整录音和转录后被The New Yorker要求撤除，但多篇实时报道保留了关键引语
**可信度**：二手（实时报道笔记），可信度：中等偏高

【他说的】被问"你讨厌哪些设计"时：
> "Most things, really."

【他说的】解释"讨厌"的原因：
> "They're built in an opportunistic way; they're built to a cost, they're built to a schedule and very often they're not built for people."
> "We're important, and we deserve at least a sincere effort."

【他说的】关于"感知到关怀"这一核心观念（他最知名的一句话之一）：
> "I believe, as a species, I believe we sense care. We find it very hard to articulate, but we genuinely sense care in the same way we sense carelessness."

【他说的】关于iPhone使用前的手机体验（隐晦地描述BlackBerry/Nokia时代）：
> They were "little soul-destroying," "flippant," and "poorly made."

【他说的】关于创意的时间节奏（对非设计师来说不可见的跳跃）：
> "On the Tuesday there's not an idea... And on Wednesday, there's an idea."

【他说的】关于Jobs教给他的专注原则：
> "Jony, you have to understand there are measures of focus, and one of them is how often you say no."
（与New Yorker 2015报道中的引用呼应，说明这是他反复提及的核心记忆）

【他说的】关于在好奇与专注之间切换的体力消耗：
> "It's exhausting... to move between" curiosity and determined focus, two opposing behaviors required for innovation.

【他说的】关于iPhone X面部识别（Face ID）：
> "That technology is something we have been looking for five years."

【记者描述的】Remnick问他对AI的看法，Ive承认"it's such a broad term that it's already here today"，但对其未来保持开放态度，没有给出明确预测。

## 6. CCA 毕业典礼演讲（2021年5月）

**来源**：California College of the Arts，2021年5月10日；由时装摄影师Nick Knight以黑白影像录制
**URL（转述）**：https://www.danielscrivner.com/articles/jony-ives-cca-commencement-address-and-advice-for-the-graduating-class
**可信度**：一手（本人演讲），可信度：高

**背景**：这是Ive离开苹果后首次发表较长的公开演讲，带有强烈的教育者/导师语气，与他在公司采访中的PR语气截然不同。

【他说的】关于创意空间的神圣性：
> "a class that permanently changed the way I thought about the sanctity of creating"
> "creating and thinking should always be afforded a rare respect"

【他说的】关于创意需要重复与深度：
> "Our professional skills develop with repetition. Our creativity develops with deep care and intention."

【他说的】关于"想法"的本质（与追悼会发言呼应）：
> "Ideas by definition, are always fragile. If they were resolved, they wouldn't be ideas."

【他说的】关于问题意识的陷阱：
> "you have to make an extraordinary effort not to focus on the problems"

【他说的】关于好奇心与学习：
> "Having a genuine relish for being surprised and for learning is fundamental to creating"
> "wanting to learn is far more important than being right"
> "Curiosity can unite us and form the basis for powerful and joyful collaborations"
> "the delight and joy of curiosity and learning can temper our fear of doing something completely new"

【他说的】关于倾听（自我反省式）：
> "Great ideas can come from the quietest voice"
> "I really worry how many good ideas I have missed because I wasn't listening"

【他说的】对毕业生的核心建议：
> "My encouragement is to be committed and proactive in caring for that deep, creative part of yourself that is easily overlooked"

【我推断的】"wanting to learn is far more important than being right"这句话在他的WSJ Jobs悼念文章中也出现，是他赋予给Jobs的金句——但在CCA演讲中他用第一人称讲，说明他已将这个原则内化为自己的信条，而不只是引用Jobs。

## 7. WSJ Magazine 封面访谈（2022年11月）

**来源**：Elisa Lipsky-Karasz，Wall Street Journal Magazine，2022年11月"创新者专辑"
**URL（转述）**：https://www.macrumors.com/2022/11/02/jony-ive-featured-on-wsj-cover/
**可信度**：二手（媒体转述），原文付费，可信度：高

【他说的】关于与Jobs的即时默契：
> "I clicked with Steve in a way that I had never before done with someone and never have since."

【他说的】关于一个将死公司的样貌：
> "The death of a company is so ugly."
（语境：回忆1990年代初加入时的苹果状态）

【他说的】关于当时加入苹果的原始动机：
> "I wanted to be a part of this crazy California company"

【他说的】关于"不可能"时的心理策略：
> "You have an idea—which is unproven and isn't resolved, since a resolved idea is a product—and the only tangible thing about the idea are the problems. When someone says it's not possible, and all you are being shown is why it's not possible, you have to think and behave in a different way. [You have to say], from a place of courage, I believe it is possible."

【他说的】关于成功与好奇心的张力：
> "Success is the enemy of curiosity."

【他说的】关于失去好奇心的恐惧：
> "I am terrified and disgusted when people are absolutely without curiosity."
> "It's at the root of so much social dysfunction and conflict."

【他说的】关于大型创意团队的矛盾本质：
> "Part of why I get so furious when people dismiss creativity is that [when] it's an activity practiced in its most noble and collaborative form, it means a bunch of people who come together in an empathic and selfless way."
> "The process of creating with large groups of people is really hard and is also unbelievably powerful."

【他说的】关于实践性格：
> "I love making things that are profoundly useful. I'm a very practical craftsperson."

【他说的】最痛苦的教训：
> "The most important lessons you would never choose to learn because they are so painful."

## 8. Stripe Sessions 与 Patrick Collison 对谈（2025年5月）

**来源**：Stripe Sessions 2025，主持人 Patrick Collison
**官方URL**：https://stripe.com/sessions/2025/a-conversation-with-sir-jony-ive
**YouTube**：https://youtu.be/wLb9g_8r-mE
**总结笔记**：https://sidneyshek.substack.com/p/notes-from-sir-jony-ives-interview
**分析文章**：https://spyglass.org/just-jony/
**可信度**：二手（多篇实时报道），视频可访问，可信度：高

**背景**：这是Ive在IO被OpenAI收购前几周接受的少见一小时深度访谈，有人称之为他迄今最开放的一次公开对话。

【他说的】关于产品与价值观的关系（核心命题）：
> "What we make stands testament to who we are. And what we make describes our values, it describes our preoccupations."

【他说的】关于什么会杀死一个想法：
> "What kills an idea is people's urge to express their opinion."

【他说的】关于设计的终极期待：
> "When people use the products you've made, they should feel that they were designed in a way to express gratitude to our species."

【他说的】关于遗漏安静声音的恐惧（自我批评式）：
> "One of the things that terrifies me, I know that I've missed really amazing ideas that came from a quiet place, from a quiet person, and that really scares me—because I don't know what I've missed."

【他说的】关于他目前最大的忧虑：
> "I think when you're innovating, of course, there will be unintended consequences. You hope that the majority will be pleasant surprises...there's probably not anything that I can be more preoccupied or bothered by than what you've just described."
（语境：被问到智能手机对青少年心理健康的影响）

【他说的】关于AI变化速度：
> "The sheer rate of change is dangerous."

【他说的】关于团队文化中的相互照顾仪式（这是少见的具体管理实践披露）：
> 他要求设计团队"每个周五上午，由一名成员为整个团队做早餐，轮流进行"。他强调，这不是为了早餐本身，而是"做一件为彼此服务的事"。

【他说的】关于硅谷精神的变质：
> "When I first moved here I came because it was characterized by people who genuinely saw that their purpose was in service to humanity, to inspire people and help people create. I don't feel that way about this place right now."

【他说的】关于包装设计中注入的关怀（用周日下午设计电缆包装的故事）：
> "Somebody gave a shit about me."（他想象用户打开包装时应有的感受）

【他说的】关于设计环境对思维的影响：
> "If you're designing for people and you're in someone's living room...of course you think differently."
> "Who would actually want to spend time in a conference room? I can't think of a more soulless and depressing place."

【他说的】关于情绪与设计结果的关系：
> "If I am anxious, you can see the anxiety in what I design."
> "To be joyful and optimistic and hopeful in our practice...is how the products will end up."

【他说的】关于极简主义的误读：
> "People think simple products are about moving out the clutter, but that just makes the product uncluttered. It just makes the product desiccated and soulless."
> "I think my goal, our goal, is to bring order to chaos."

【他说的】关于当下有未披露的工作（暗示OpenAI项目）：
> "I can't talk about it at the moment."（被问到当前在做什么时）

【他说的】关于不为破坏而破坏：
> "I have no interest in breaking stuff for the sake of breaking stuff."
> "Breaking stuff and moving on quickly leaves us surrounded by carnage."

【记者描述的/分析者观察的】Ive在整个访谈中系统性地回避了对ROI或商业可量化指标的讨论，每当Patrick Collison试图引导到效率或数据时，Ive都把话题拉回到哲学和人的感受层面。他用"我相信……虽然我没有实证数据"这种表达方式承认这一点：
> "I do believe, and I wish that I had empirical evidence."

## 9. Financial Times + Laurene Powell Jobs 联合专访（2025年6月）

**来源**：Financial Times，2025年6月，采访背景为IO被OpenAI收购
**URL（转述）**：https://9to5mac.com/2025/06/02/jony-ive-and-laurene-powell-jobs-admit-to-dark-side-of-tech-in-joint-interview/
**可信度**：二手（媒体转述），原文付费，可信度：高

**背景**：这是LoveFrom被OpenAI以64亿美元收购前后最重要的一次访谈，也是Ive与Laurene Powell Jobs首次联合公开亮相。

【他说的】关于造成伤害的责任感：
> "If you make something new, if you innovate, there will be consequences unforeseen, and some will be wonderful and some will be harmful. While some of the less positive consequences were unintentional, I still feel responsibility. And the manifestation of that is a determination to try and be useful."

【他说的】关于硅谷失去人文使命感（与Stripe Sessions发言基本一致，是他在多个场合重复的核心判断）：
> "When I first moved here I came because it was characterized by people who genuinely saw that their purpose was in service to humanity...I don't feel that way about this place right now."

【他说的】关于LoveFrom的起源：
> "If it wasn't for Laurene, there wouldn't be LoveFrom."

【他说的】关于OpenAI项目重燃的乐观：
（被记者归结为）设备设计理念是："we deserve better. Humanity deserves better."

【Laurene Powell Jobs 说的】关于科技的黑暗面（供参照）：
> "We now know, unambiguously, that there are dark uses for certain types of technology. You can only look at the studies being done on teenage girls and on anxiety in young people, and the rise of mental health needs, to understand that we've gone sideways."

【记者描述的】在被问及OpenAI设备是否会与苹果竞争时，Ive和Powell Jobs均未正面回应，措辞模糊。这是本次访谈中他刻意回避的话题之一。

## 17. 他拒绝或回避的问题类型

【我推断的，基于多次访谈的系统性观察】

以下话题在采访记录中呈现出明显的"模糊化"或沉默：

1. **对Apple Watch销量/商业成绩的看法**：每次被问到产品表现，他都把话题拉回"对的事情"，而非商业数字。
2. **对离开苹果决定的内部细节**：他从不直接解释为什么走，只说"curiosity"。Parker的2015报道也没能从他嘴里得到直接的时间线。
3. **对苹果当前设计质量的评价**：2025年以后的访谈中，他一概用"他们是好人"之类中性表述代替具体评价。
4. **OpenAI设备的具体形态**：在Financial Times访谈中刻意回避。
5. **对个人财务的任何讨论**：在已知的所有访谈中，他从未主动提及薪酬、股权或财富。
6. **对Walter Isaacson Jobs传记的详细批评**：他在New Yorker 2015中说"My regard couldn't be any lower"，但此后未再公开扩展这一评论。
7. **与Scott Forstall关系**（iOS 7重设计中的内部权力角力）：从未在任何采访中触及。

## 18. 他改变立场或承认错误的罕见时刻

【他说的】承认自己没有跟从Jobs"不要想我会怎么做"的遗嘱：
> "I'm doing exactly what you didn't want."（BBC Desert Island Discs，2025）

【他说的】承认智能手机带来的"不那么正面的后果"，虽是非预期的，但他感到个人责任：
> "While some of the less positive consequences were unintentional, I still feel responsibility."（Financial Times，2025）

【他说的】承认自己可能错失了来自安静成员的好想法（对自己领导力的批评）：
> "I really worry how many good ideas I have missed because I wasn't listening."（CCA 演讲，2021）

【记者描述的】在New Yorker 2015报道中，Parker注意到Ive以"unusual heat"回应Isaacson传记的问题——这是他极少数公开表现出情绪化的时刻，显示Jobs的遗产对他有极深的个人情感负载，并非只是PR叙事。

【我推断的】Ive关于"minimalism"的立场在2025年的Stripe Sessions中发生了微妙转变：他此前多年用"真正的简洁"来捍卫iOS 7和后续产品的极简美学，但在2025年他开始批评"soulless and desiccated"的设计——这暗示他对自己主导的部分设计决策感到了距离感，尽管他没有点名任何具体产品。
