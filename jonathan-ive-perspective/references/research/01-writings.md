## Repeated core claims (>=3 recurrences)

1) Design is about care in use, not decorative styling.
- Evidence A (primary): Design Museum Q&A: "The defining qualities are about use: ease and simplicity."
- Evidence B (primary): Design Museum Q&A: "fanatical care beyond the obvious stuff".
- Evidence C (primary): Apple 2019 statement emphasizes long-term design process and culture, not single visual trend.
- Confidence: High (primary direct language).

2) Simplicity is produced by deep technical work in materials/process, not by removing features on slides.
- Evidence A (primary): Design Museum Q&A discusses polymers, co-moulding, laser welding, and architecture decisions.
- Evidence B (primary): Q&A describes iPod enclosure approach and dense sealed construction.
- Evidence C (secondary): Wikipedia repeatedly links Ive work to Rams/Bauhaus influenced reduction.
- Confidence: High for principle, Medium for historical framing from Wikipedia.

3) Great product work requires cross-functional alignment and a protected small team.
- Evidence A (primary): Design Museum Q&A: design effectiveness depends on leadership + development + marketing + sales sharing goals.
- Evidence B (primary): Design Museum Q&A: keep core team small and invest in tools/process.
- Evidence C (primary): Apple 2019 statement: Ive proudest of design team/process/culture built over 30 years.
- Confidence: High.

4) Product meaning includes emotional legibility, not only utility.
- Evidence A (primary): Design Museum Q&A on iMac handle: functional plus psychological invitation to touch and move.
- Evidence B (primary): repeated language around responsibility and intimacy of frequently used devices.
- Evidence C (secondary): Wikipedia and museum profile frame his impact through humanizing personal technology.
- Confidence: Medium-High.

## Signature concepts and terms seen in corpus
